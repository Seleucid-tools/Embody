[size=4][b]Description[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Embody gives your character a visible body in first person. Look down and you'll see your chest, arms, legs, and feet — present in the world, not floating behind an invisible camera.
The idea is simple: Skyrim's first-person camera stays exactly as Bethesda made it, and your body is moved each frame to follow it. The camera's feel doesn't change — no added head-bob, no reinvented controls — you just gain a body underneath it. This is the approach the original Enhanced Camera took on the old engine, re-derived from scratch for Skyrim Special Edition and Anniversary Edition.
[b]Why release at version 0.54?[/b] Because the core is stable and genuinely enjoyable today — your body shows correctly while standing and sneaking, weapon drawn or sheathed, through room transitions, spellcasting, and menus — but it isn't the complete Enhanced Camera experience yet (see Planned Features). I'd rather share a solid, working piece now and build the rest in the open. If you try it, I'd love your feedback: what feels right, what doesn't, what breaks, and what you'd want prioritized. Bug reports that list your other camera and animation mods are especially useful.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Installation[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Install with your mod manager (Mod Organizer 2, Vortex) like any other mod, or unzip the archive into your Skyrim Special Edition folder so the files land under Data/. Launch the game through SKSE.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Main Features[/b][/size]
[list]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]A visible first-person body[/b] — chest, arms, legs, and feet, planted on the ground.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Your vanilla camera, untouched[/b] — no head-bob added, no camera reinvention; stock first-person feel.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Weapon-aware arms[/b] — crisp first-person arms when your weapon is drawn, your own body's arms when it's sheathed.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Magic shaders on the body[/b] — Stoneflesh, Muffle, and similar effects render on the visible body.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Stays put[/b] — the body doesn't vanish at room transitions, on stairs, mid-cast, or when you switch POV. (This was the hard part.)[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Fully tunable[/b] — per-stance body offsets, body scale, and anchor mode, adjustable live through an MCM or a plain INI, with changes applied on the fly.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Update-resilient[/b] — resolves engine addresses through Address Library, so it survives most game updates automatically.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Framework-light[/b] — one small SKSE plugin, no heavy dependencies.[/color][/size][/*]
[/list]

[size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]How Embody differs from Improved Camera SE[/b]
Improved Camera SE is an excellent mod and the more mature, more complete option today — if you want the widest coverage right now, it's a great choice. Embody isn't a replacement so much as a different approach, and which you prefer really comes down to taste:[/color][/size]
[list]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Improved Camera[/b] moves the camera to match your animated body.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Embody[/b] moves the body to match the vanilla camera.[/color][/size][/*]
[/list]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Improved Camera's approach already covers situations Embody doesn't yet (horseback, transformations, killmoves, scripted scenes). Embody's approach leaves the stock first-person camera exactly as-is, which some players prefer for its unchanged feel. Same goal, approached from opposite ends — pick whichever suits how you like first person to feel. The two do the same job, so run only one at a time.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Planned Features[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Right now Embody shows your body in the states that are already first-person (standing and sneaking). The larger goal — full Enhanced Camera parity — is to also bring first person to states that are normally third-person, roughly in this order:[/color][/size]
[list]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]First person while sitting and using furniture[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]First person at crafting stations (forge, alchemy, enchanting, and so on)[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]First person while mounted (horseback)[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]First person during killmoves (off by default, so cinematic third-person kills stay intact)[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]First person as Werewolf and Vampire Lord[/color][/size][/*]
[/list]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Some of these already appear as placeholder toggles in the MCM so the plumbing is in place — they'll switch on as each one lands. Feedback on what to prioritize is welcome.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Known Limitations (it's an early release)[/b][/size]
[list]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]A brief one-frame flicker when opening or closing the Tween menu (TAB). Minor; the body is stable otherwise.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]The body's shadow is currently headless (the head is hidden by scaling). Being reworked.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8]The third-person states listed under Planned Features still play in vanilla third person for now — Embody simply steps aside in those, so nothing misbehaves.[/color][/size][/*]
[/list]

[size=4][b]How to Use[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]With the MCM[/b] (needs SkyUI + MCM Helper): open the pause menu → Mod Configuration → Embody. Four pages:[/color][/size]
[list]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Body Tuning[/b] — anchor mode, body scale, and per-stance offset sliders. This is the one most people need.[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Feature Toggles[/b] — the planned first-person states above (placeholders for now).[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Transform Tuning[/b] — Werewolf / Vampire Lord offsets (for a future feature).[/color][/size][/*]
[*][size=3][color=#D4D4D8][b]Controls[/b] — optional live-tuning hotkeys, off by default; enable them here if you want to nudge the body in real time.[/color][/size][/*]
[/list]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Dial the Body Tuning offsets until your body sits right for your character and animations. Changes apply live.
[b]Without the MCM:[/b] Embody still works fully. It reads Data/MCM/Settings/Embody.ini directly and reloads it live while the game runs — edit the values (each is documented in the settings.ini that ships with the mod), save, and the body updates within a second. The file is created for you on first launch.[/color][/size]
